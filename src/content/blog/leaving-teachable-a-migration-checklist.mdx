## Test access before you send the announcement

The email announcing your new platform should be the last step, not the first. Before it goes out, one buyer who already paid should be able to log into the new home and find everything they own. Most Teachable migrations that go badly go badly here: the content moved, but a customer's purchase history and access didn't, and now support is untangling it in public.

Teachable is built to get a course selling fast, and it's good at that. What it isn't built for is handing you a clean, complete copy of your business on the way out. You can export student and transaction data as CSV. You generally can't click one button and receive your videos, your course structure, and every learner's exact access state in a portable bundle. So the plan is: move the content, then rebuild the *state* around each customer.

## The checklist, in the order that protects customers

- **Export students and transactions first.** Pull the CSVs while you still have access. Names, emails, purchase dates, products owned, amounts. This is the spine of the migration.
- **Inventory the content you'll re-host.** Videos and files usually need to be re-uploaded to the new platform or a media host you control. Catalogue every lesson and its assets so nothing goes missing between systems.
- **Rebuild the access map.** For each customer, which products should they own on day one? A learner who finished six modules should not land on an empty dashboard.
- **Preserve completion where it matters.** If a certificate or a "you finished" state affects a customer, decide how that carries over before, not after, the switch.
- **Re-create the checkout and test a real purchase.** Run a live buy on the new platform end to end: payment, receipt, account creation, access. Then test a refund.
- **Plan the redirect and the login story.** People will have the old URL bookmarked. Decide what happens when they land there.
- **Then, and only then, announce.**

## The line that actually matters

Here's the test I use before signing off on a course migration: pick three real customers, one brand new, one halfway through, one who finished, and log in as each on the new platform. If all three see exactly what they paid for, you're ready. If any of them see less, the announcement waits.
