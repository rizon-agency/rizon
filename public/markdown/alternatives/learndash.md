# LearnDash / WordPress LMS alternative

**Comparison page:** [/alternatives/learndash](/alternatives/learndash)
**Related service:** [/services/custom-lms-development](/services/custom-lms-development)

LearnDash can be sensible when a WordPress site and courses belong together. A dedicated LMS becomes worthwhile when every learner or checkout change starts with a compatibility check across WordPress, a theme, commerce, membership, cache, and add-ons.

## Why teams leave

- Plugin dependency chains make release risk hard to see.
- Performance work has too many moving parts.
- The operational learning product needs a clearer boundary from editorial publishing.

## Where LearnDash wins

Keep it for a small, well-managed WordPress catalogue or when WordPress publishing should remain tightly coupled to courses.

## Comparison

| Dimension | Rizon | LearnDash / WordPress |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Release process | One application process tailored to the product | WordPress core, theme, LearnDash, and plugins interact |
| Commerce | Built around chosen payment and access rules | Usually relies on WordPress commerce or membership tooling |
| Ownership | Dedicated codebase and handover | WordPress site plus selected plugins |

## Migration and FAQ

Audit WordPress content, LearnDash courses, users, purchases, and add-ons before production changes. Marketing content can often remain in WordPress while the operational LMS moves to its own product. A focused release is generally **8–12 weeks**.
