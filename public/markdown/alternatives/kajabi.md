# Kajabi alternative for an owned course platform

**Comparison page:** [/alternatives/kajabi](/alternatives/kajabi)
**Related service:** [/services/course-platform-for-creators](/services/course-platform-for-creators)

Kajabi's all-in-one approach is valuable until the learning experience becomes distinct enough that one suite decides too much. Do not rebuild because a button is wrong; rebuild when a permanent workaround sits in selling, delivery, or support.

## Why creators leave

- One suite can make specialist tools and a distinct member experience harder to choose.
- Course, account, and community journeys need their own interface.
- Payments, CRM, analytics, and email need choices outside a bundled subscription.

## Where Kajabi wins

Kajabi is practical for teams that want products, marketing pages, and email in one managed service, especially when the catalogue is still simple.

## Comparison

| Dimension | Rizon | Kajabi |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stack choices | Pick tools around your requirements | Uses Kajabi's suite and supported connections |
| Member experience | Designed for your programme and brand | Configured inside existing patterns |
| Operations | Code and deployment handover | Hosted subscription product with vendor-managed updates |

## Migration and FAQ

Map products, offers, members, media, and third-party connections. The first release covers critical purchase and learner journeys; low-value automations are reviewed instead of copied by reflex. A focused replacement usually launches in **8–12 weeks**.
