# Canvas alternative for schools that need control

**Comparison page:** [/alternatives/canvas](/alternatives/canvas)
**Related service:** [/services/school-student-portal-development](/services/school-student-portal-development)

Canvas is a strong fit for an institution that wants a mature hosted LMS with limited engineering involvement. A custom alternative is for a school whose learner, parent, and staff experience is part of its offer and no longer fits a vendor roadmap.

## Why teams consider a change

- Student, parent, and staff workflows need a shared portal rather than an LMS-only experience.
- The institution needs direct control of the product roadmap and interaction design.
- Learning data must connect to SIS, services, or institutional processes in a specific way.
- Canvas exports are not a complete migration plan: grades, student interactions, and some external-tool configurations require separate handling.

## Where Canvas wins

Canvas remains sensible when the existing course model works, vendor-managed operations matter, and an established higher-ed/K–12 LMS is the actual need.

## Comparison

| Dimension | Rizon | Canvas |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Code | Institution receives the application and handover | Vendor-operated hosted LMS |
| Learner experience | Built around institutional journeys | Configured within Canvas patterns |
| Integrations | Designed around required SSO, SIS, LTI, and services | Supported product integrations and institutional configuration |
| Roadmap | Set by the institution's business case | Shared vendor roadmap and product settings |

## Migration and FAQ

Start with a system map: courses, identities, SIS data, tools, roles, and high-volume journeys. A custom build can support SSO, SIS, and LTI when their versions and launch flows are confirmed in discovery. Replacing Canvas is realistic only with a precise case and owners for the workflows being changed.
