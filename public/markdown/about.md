# About Rizon

## I build the learning platform, so it stops being the thing your team works around.

I’m Choaib Mouhrach. I started Rizon to build learning platforms for course businesses, schools, and training teams whose software no longer matches how they actually teach.

![Choaib Mouhrach](https://rizon.agency/choaib-mouhrach-pic.png)

## How Rizon started

My first job out of university was at a public school. The learning system they’d been handed looked fine in a demo and fell apart in daily use. Teachers kept a parallel spreadsheet because they trusted it more than the software. The tool that was supposed to save time had quietly become another thing to manage.

Around then I joined a learning-development agency, and the LMS work pulled me in deeper than I expected: LTI, Moodle, Canvas, the standards that hold these systems together, and the specific places they break. I spent a few years learning where learning software actually fails: not the headline feature, but the exception nobody planned for. The cohort that needs a different rule. The parent who needs a view. The completion record someone has to trust a year later.

When I left, I couldn’t unsee the pattern. It wasn’t one bad product. Course creators, schools, and training teams were all fighting the same gap between how they teach and what their platform allows. So I started Rizon to close it, by building the platform around the teaching instead of the other way round. The [Kaiser Math Platform](work/kaiser-math-platform.md) is one of those builds: courses, quizzes, points, and an admin side, made for one team’s way of teaching rather than bent out of a template.

That work is less glamorous than a launch video. It’s also where a platform either earns its keep or becomes the next thing people route around.

## The way I work

### Small releases. Direct conversations. Code you keep.

Projects move in weekly slices with live reviews, so you catch awkward paths before they turn into expensive assumptions. At handover, the code and deployment docs are yours. I’ll stay on for support when it helps, but Rizon isn’t built to trap anyone in a retainer, and I’ll tell you when a problem is really a configuration fix, not a build.

Bring the part of the platform that’s making the work harder. We’ll separate a solvable configuration problem from a product problem worth building, before you spend a cent on either.
